Japanese food are not only tasty, but also look pretty. The traditional foods are rice, ramen, miso soup, fish, vegetable, etc. The sushi, with raw fish on top of the rice, is also very popular in Japan. Since Japan is an island nation which is surrounded by an ocean, it has abundant supply of fresh seafood. The typical seasonings include dashi, soy sauce, mirin, vinegar, sugar and salt. The Japapense cooking techniques are raw, grilled, simmered, steamed, deep-fried and vinegared. Japanese cuisine are beautiful and delicious!

The Taiwanese cuisine always includes pork, seafood, chicken, rice, noodles and baos. One of the very well-known dishes is beef noodle soup. If you have a chance to go to Taiwan, you must try this beef noodle soup. Taiwan is also an island nation surrounding by the ocean, so it has a abundant supply of seafood such as tuna, sardines, octopus, oysters and cuttlefish. Taiwanese people love fresh ingredients and always cook with soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, fermented black beans, cilantro and basil. Taiwan is famous for its night markets, and there are a lot of street food stalls in these night markets. Not only tourists would go there, but a lot of local residents would eat there too. Taiwanese food are yummy!

The Korean meals always have rice, vegetables and meats. They also have a lot of side dishes such as kimchi and other marinated vegetables. They usually cook with sesame oil, fermented bean paste, soy sauce, pepper, salt, garlic, and red chili paste. They love spicy hot dishes, so if you do not like spicy food, don't forget to tell the waiters to make your dishes not spicy. A cold noodle dish with a broth is also very well-known for the Korean cuisine. Korean usually steam, stew, boil and smoke the food, and the Korean BBQ is very popular in Korea. Korean food are very special!
